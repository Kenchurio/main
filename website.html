Information Systems vs Information Technology

Information Systems (IS) and Information Technology (IT) are interrelated yet distinct concepts in the realm of computing. Information Systems refer to an integrated set of components working collaboratively to gather, process, store, and disseminate information to support decision-making in an organizational context.

It encompasses hardware, software, data, procedures, and human elements, focusing on optimizing information usage to achieve organizational goals. On the other hand, Information Technology encompasses a broader spectrum, including hardware, software, networks, and various technologies employed for acquiring, storing, and processing information.

While IS emphasizes the organizational context and effective information utilization, IT is a more extensive term covering the technological tools and infrastructure for managing information across diverse domains. In essence, IS provides the structured framework for employing IT tools to enhance information management within organizations.
